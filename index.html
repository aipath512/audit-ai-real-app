Proof Terms Explained

SHA-256 — Digital Fingerprint

Simple meaning: A unique digital fingerprint for a file, document or definition.

Why it matters: If one single character changes, the fingerprint changes. This helps prove that the document has not been altered.

OTS / OpenTimestamps — Proof of Date

Simple meaning: A timestamp proof showing that a file existed at a specific moment.

Why it matters: It helps prove prior existence of an innovation, definition, policy or evidence file.

Bitcoin Block — Public Time Anchor

Simple meaning: A public, hard-to-change timestamp anchor.

Why it matters: Once a proof is anchored, nobody can easily rewrite the date later.
